🎯 DIALYSIS CENTER CRITICAL POINTS

PIC Requirements
Nephrologist OR Internal Medicine + 200hrs training + Nephrologist affiliation. Valid APC & NSR required.
Staffing Mandate
Centre Manager: Post-basic renal nursing + 2yrs experience. All nurses: 6 months renal training minimum.
Infection Control
Blood-borne screening (HBV, HCV, HIV), Designated infection control staff, CRBSI surveillance, Fistula care.
Water Quality
AAMI 2015/ISO 23500:2014 standards. Daily chlorine testing, monthly endotoxin, 6-monthly chemical testing.
Clinical Requirements
Nephrologist review every 3 months, Prescription documentation, Outcome monitoring, Patient education.
Mandatory Reporting
MOH notification: Viral seroconversion, Intradialytic deaths, Pyogenic reactions, NRR compliance certificate.

⚠️ CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS FOR DIALYSIS CENTERS

100% CORE Compliance
Zero tolerance - all 36 standards are mandatory for patient safety
Documentation Heavy
Daily logs, monthly tests, quarterly reviews, incident reports
Specialized Expertise
Nephrology knowledge, renal nursing, infection control training
Equipment Critical
Water treatment, dialysis machines, maintenance, calibration
BOTTOM LINE: Dialysis centers have the highest compliance requirements - ALL standards are CORE and patient safety critical.

🎯 EMERGENCY DEPARTMENT FOCUS AREAS

Triage System
Malaysian Triage Category (MTC): Red (immediate), Yellow (≤30 min), Green (≤90 min). Must have trained triage personnel.
Essential Equipment
Airway management, oxygen delivery, ventilation support, vascular access, defibrillator, monitoring equipment, point-of-care diagnostics.
Emergency Medications
Resuscitation cart drugs: Adrenaline, Atropine, Amiodarone, Lignocaine, Glucose 50%, Naloxone, Flumazenil. Floor stock: Antihypertensives, bronchodilators.
Performance Indicators
Mandatory: Inappropriate triaging (≤0.5%). Waiting times by category. Unplanned 24-hour returns. Track and trend all indicators.
Head of Department
Must be Emergency Medicine specialist (NSR registered) OR visiting specialist supervision. Valid APC required.
Patient Transfer
Proper referral communication, qualified escorts, continuity of care, appropriate transport, complete documentation.

⚱️ MORTUARY SERVICES KEY REQUIREMENTS

Service Supervision
Medical practitioner supervision required. State head of forensic medicine oversight. Training in mortuary processes essential.
Essential Areas
Body reception, storage (2-8°C), preparation/release, viewing, bereavement room, post-mortem suite (where applicable).
Documentation Requirements
Body registration, specimen forwarding, evidence records, forensic reports filing, movement tracking. Chain of evidence maintained.
Performance Indicators
Turnaround time ≤3hrs (≥80%), Correct body release (≥99%), PM report completion within 12 weeks (≥80%).
Safety & Equipment
PPE provision, specialized equipment training, MSDS documentation, post-mortem equipment, temperature monitoring.
Policies Required
Post-mortem procedures, specimen handling, safety protocols, body management, religious rites accommodation.

🛡️ INFECTION CONTROL & AMS CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

HIACC Committee
Medical practitioner chair, multi-disciplinary members, 6-monthly meetings minimum. PCI & AMS teams report to HIACC.
ICN Qualifications
Post-basic/advanced diploma infection control training required. AMS pharmacist needs AMS training program certification.
Essential Policies
Aseptic techniques, PPE use, HAI isolation, antimicrobial policy, sterilization, housekeeping, waste management, facility maintenance.
PCI Performance Indicators
Staff training (85%), HAI rate (≤5%), MDRO surveillance (MRSA ≤0.3%, ESBL ≤0.2-0.3%, CRE ≤0.1%), VRE (≤0.1%).
AMS Performance Indicators
Complete prescriptions documentation, appropriate antimicrobial prescribing per guidelines, 72-hour empirical prescription review.
Facility Requirements
Adequate PPE, hand hygiene facilities, isolation rooms (pressure monitoring), manufacturer compliance for devices/disinfectants.

🛡️ INFECTION CONTROL & ANTIMICROBIAL STEWARDSHIP FOCUS

HIACC Structure
Medical chair, multi-disciplinary, 6-monthly meetings, PCI & AMS reporting
Trained Personnel
ICN post-basic trained, AMS pharmacist certified, link nurses in all wards
Comprehensive Policies
Infection control, antimicrobial policy, facility-wide implementation
Performance Monitoring
HAI rates, MDRO surveillance, antimicrobial prescribing patterns
BOTTOM LINE: Infection control is facility-wide responsibility requiring structured governance, trained personnel, and continuous monitoring.
